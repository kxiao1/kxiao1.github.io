About Me

My name is Karl Xiao (肖泽天/萧澤天), and I am a Junior studying Computational Finance and Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University. Born in Shanghai, China and raised in Singapore, I opted to pursue further studies in the United States after completing Singapore's mandatory military service. This past summer, I completed a front-end internship in software engineering at SEA Group (NYSE: SE) . I am looking for Summer 2021 opportunities in software engineering and quantitative trading.

After hopping among several colleges, I decided on the CF/CS track because I saw the immense value in being able to compute something (which is why both my Majors have that word in it). Much of the credit is due to those dedicated and erudite professors I've had the pleasure to learn under, who with complete mastery of their areas of expertise illuminated the limitless potential of math and technology to solve the world's problems. During my Freshman Summer, I did research in Mathematical Finance on the pricing of perpetual options, and my group used both mathematical proofs and numerical approximation for corroboration. In my Sophomore year, I took several probabilistic and calculus-based advanced classes which shed new light on this earlier research. I was also fortunate to serve as a Course Tutor for 15-251 Great Theoretical Ideas in Computer Science, a primer for the study of computation, in Spring 2020. In 2020-21, my goal is to get comfortable with big data and big systems, which I believe are advancing the frontiers of human knowledge as we speak.

Outside of work, my greatest passion is exploring different places and cultures, whether it's nearby neighborhoods or distant countries. When physical travel is not possible (for instance, during the ongoing coronavirus pandemic), film, music, and literature have provided invaluable respite. As some sort of corollary, I am also an avid learner of foreign languages. These have helped me better engage with the locals wherever I go. Owing to my immigrant upbringing, I speak English and Mandarin Chinese with a native proficiency. While French is my best "foreign language", I also speak/ understand bits and pieces of German, Spanish, Shanghainese, and Cantonese due to travel and cultural exposure. If you're at CMU and want to know more aboue languages, I strongly recommend 80-180 Nature of Language. As a Francophile, I am also excited to take 82-303 French & Francophone Cultures in Spring 2021.
