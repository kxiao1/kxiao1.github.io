About Me

My name is Karl Xiao (肖泽天/萧澤天), and I graduated in May 2022 from Carnegie Mellon University with degrees in Computational Finance and Computer Science. Born in Shanghai, China and raised in Singapore, I opted to attend college in the United States after completing mandatory military service in Singapore. I am currently a full-time Quantitative Analyst at Citigroup. Previous internships include full-stack Spring Boot development at Xandr (acquired by Microsoft in Dec 2021) in Summer 2021, and a front-end ReactJS stint at SEA Group (NYSE: SE) in Summer 2020.

After hopping from college to college, I decided on the CF/CS track because I saw the immense value in being able to compute something. Thanks to the dedication and passion of Carnegie Mellon's amazing professors, I am privileged to have caught a glimpse of the wondrous potential of math and technology during my four years there. As a Math Major, I enjoy the mathematical beauty of Algebra and Analysis, especially their applications to cryptography, math finance, and beyond. On the tech side, my interests span Machine Learning, Distributed Systems, Databases, and Web Development.

Outside of work, my greatest passion is exploring different places and cultures, be they nearby neighborhoods or distant countries. When physical travel is not possible (for instance, during the ongoing coronavirus pandemic), film, music, and literature have provided invaluable respite. Complementing that, I like learning languages to better understand cultures. Owing to my immigrant upbringing, I am a native speaker of English and Mandarin Chinese. French is my best "foreign" language, but I have also picked up bits and pieces of German, Spanish, Shanghainese, and Cantonese from travelling and self-study. If you're at CMU and want to know more about languages, I strongly recommend 80-180 Nature of Language. As a Francophile, I also thoroughly enjoyed 82-303 French & Francophone Cultures in Spring 2021.
