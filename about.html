About Me

My name is Karl Xiao (肖泽天/萧澤天), and I just graduated from Carnegie Mellon University with degrees in Computational Finance and Computer Science. Born in Shanghai, China and raised in Singapore, I opted to attend college in the United States after completing mandatory military service in Singapore. I will soon be joining Citi as a full-time Quantitative Analyst. Previous work experience includes full-stack Spring Boot development at Xandr (recently acquired by Microsoft!) in Summer 2021, and a front-end ReactJS internship at SEA Group (NYSE: SE) in Summer 2020.

After hopping from college to college, I decided on the CF/CS track because I saw the immense value in being able to compute something. I'm privileged to have studied under dedicated and knowledgeable professors, whose expertise illuminated the limitless potential of math and technology to solve the world's problems. As a Math Major, I enjoy the mathematical beauty of Algebra and Analysis, especially their applications to cryptography, math finance, and beyond. On the tech side, my interests span Machine Learning, Distributed Systems, Databases, and Web Development. By leveraging large-scale systems and innovative algorithms, I believe that value can be unlocked for individuals and firms alike.

Outside of work, my greatest passion is exploring different places and cultures, be they nearby neighborhoods or distant countries. When physical travel is not possible (for instance, during the ongoing coronavirus pandemic), film, music, and literature have provided invaluable respite. Complementing that, I like learning languages to better understand cultures. Owing to my immigrant upbringing, I am a native speaker of English and Mandarin Chinese. While French is my best "foreign" language, I have also picked up bits and pieces of German, Spanish, Shanghainese, and Cantonese from travelling and self-study. If you're at CMU and want to know more about languages, I strongly recommend 80-180 Nature of Language. As a Francophile, I also thoroughly enjoyed 82-303 French & Francophone Cultures in Spring 2021.

What I Hope to Do Here

My overarching goal for this website is to present to the reader what I find meaningful about my life. I have included sections discussing my academic involvement, projects, and travels throughout my time at CMU. If you can read Chinese, I also have a small page with some of my Chinese writings from a long time ago. While I am not likely to update this website regularly anymore, I hope that visitors, especially undergrduates or those from CMU, will find something worth reading here.
