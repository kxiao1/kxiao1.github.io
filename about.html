About Me

My name is Karl Xiao (肖泽天/萧澤天), and I am a Senior studying Computational Finance and Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University. Born in Shanghai, China and raised in Singapore, I opted to attend college in the United States after completing Singapore's mandatory military service. I will be joining Citi as a full-time Quantitative Analyst upon my graduation in Spring 2022. For Summer 2021, I did full-stack Spring Boot development for my internship at Xandr. In Summer 2020, I completed a front-end internship in software engineering at SEA Group (NYSE: SE).

After hopping among several colleges, I decided on the CF/CS track because I saw the immense value in being able to compute something. I'm privileged to have studied under dedicated and knowledgeable professors, whose expertise illuminated the limitless potential of math and technology to solve the world's problems. As a Math Major, I enjoy the mathematical beauty in Algebra and Analysis, especially their applications to cryptography, math finance, and beyond. On the tech side, my interests span Machine Learning, Distributed Systems, Databases, and Web Development. By leveraging large-scale systems and innovative algorithms, I believe that value can be unlocked for individuals and firms alike.

Outside of work, my greatest passion is exploring different places and cultures, whether it's nearby neighborhoods or distant countries. When physical travel is not possible (for instance, during the ongoing coronavirus pandemic), film, music, and literature have provided invaluable respite. Complementing that, I am also an avid learner of foreign languages, which have connected me with foreign cultures. Owing to my immigrant upbringing, I speak English and Mandarin Chinese with a native proficiency. While French is my best "foreign language", I have also picked up bits and pieces of German, Spanish, Shanghainese, and Cantonese from travelling and self-study. If you're at CMU and want to know more about languages, I strongly recommend 80-180 Nature of Language. As a Francophile, I also thoroughly enjoyed 82-303 French & Francophone Cultures in Spring 2021.
