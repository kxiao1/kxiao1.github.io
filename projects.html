2D Chess (Winter 2020)

Inspired by the Netflix show The Queen's Gambit, I decided to study in a bit more detail some of the most celebrated chess games in history, such as the Opera Game and Fischer's Game of the Century. Unfortunately, I do not possess Beth's ability to visualize chess games on the ceiling. Therefore, I built a offline simulator that allows the player to explore, in theory, every single legal game. With move highlighting, the ability to undo, restart, or resign, as well as the outputing of game logs with algebraic notation, this desktop app is ideal for practicing chess. The program was developed in Java (using the JavaFX library), and built with Maven. The complete code can be found here.

Visual Question Answering (Fall 2020)

Imagine that you are visually impaired and can't really discern the expiry date of your bread, or more annoyingly the card number on your new credit card. Over the past few years, large strides have been made in Visual Question Answering (VQA) such that a blind person can take a picture of their surroundings, ask a question, upload them, and have a machine analyze the image for an apposite answer. This task nonetheless remains difficult because we must deal with poor-quality images and often inconsistent human-annotated training examples. In this project, we built upon a successful Attention-based CNN baseline using image augmentation, feature identification, and a refined attention model. Our best-performing model improved accuracy by 3.9% relative to the baseline.

Forex Options Research Project (Summer 2019)

We conducted an interesting study of FOREX options in perpetual time. With finite-time American call options, for instance, one will not exercise them early when the prevailing domestic interest rate \(r_d\) is positive. But in the FOREX case, a delay in exercising means losing out on the foreign currency's interest rate \(r_f\). Suppose further that one is given unlimited time to make up his mind. How then to proceed? We determine criteria for optimally exercising perpetual FOREX options - this exercise threshold would be some "stock price" (to be precise, some exchange rate) and we can plot the option's price \(V(S)\) against the underlying price. Complementing the stationary Black-Scholes ODE \[\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2S^2\frac{d^2V}{d^2S} + (r_d - r_f) S \frac{dV}{dS} - r_d V(s) = 0\] with extensive numerical simulation, we obtained various results which can be seen as generalizations of their finite, long-maturity corollaries. Stock Market Tracking (Summer 2018)

Before coming to CMU, I built an Excel Spreadsheet that could provide real-time alerts to price fluctuations, similar to the push notifications that Robinhood sends to your phone. The main difference is that with Robinhood, you see the price movement but you cannot manipulate the data, for instance put it in a chart or record it in a specific table. My spreadsheet uses Excel's Get and Transform Tool (previously known as Power Query) to periodically grab tables of data from a Google Sheet. Upon every refresh, the Google Sheet contained up-to-date information thanks to Google Finance. Lastly, I used VBA scripts to automate the routine information processing.
