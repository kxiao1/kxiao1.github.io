Activity Descriptions

15-251 Tutor

15-251 is the introductory course in Theoretical Computer Science at CMU. It introduces concepts such as Turing Machines, decidability, Graph Theory, P vs NP, and Cryptography. As a tutor, I meet 1-on-1 with a student every week to review that week's content and reinforce his understanding of the key concepts.

SSA Treasurer

As the treasurer of the Singapore Students Association, I take full responsibility of the organization's finances. A large part of my work is formulating a $6,000+ annual budget and overseeing its disbursement throughout the year. We organize activites to foster team-building, celebrate local festivals, and promote Singaporean culture to the wider community.

Moneythink Mentor

During my stint at Moneythink CMU as Mentor Captain, I conducted weekly 1-hour mentorships in different Pittsburgh-area High Schools on financial management. We regularly delivered Powerpoint presentations and academic materials. Topics covered include Savings, Credit Cards, Loans, Taxes, Investment, College Admissions, and Insurance.

Consulting Club

I served as a Pro-Bono Lead in 2019 at CMU's Undergraduate Consulting Club. So far, I have participated in 2 pro-bono consulting projects. Currently, we liaise with a multinational Healthcare startup for an ongoing project on Indian and U.S. market research. Previously, we aided a Pittsburgh-based educational non-profit’s sponsor acquisition efforts in 2018-19 by evaluating potential partners and creating a long-term framework for sponsorship retention.

Technical Enhanced Facilities Operator

As a TEF Operator, I performed Level 1 technical support to resolve software issues faced by users of Carnegie Mellon’s Windows-, Mac- and Linux-based computing terminals for 8+ hours per week during term time.

Rowing

I was part of CMU's club rowing team during my first Fall semester. The sport taught me much about grit, team chemistry, and coordination, and I enjoyed watching my blade graze the calm waters. Unfortunately, I decided to quit due to physical exhaustion and busier subsequent semesters.
