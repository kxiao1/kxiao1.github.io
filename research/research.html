Summer 2019 Research

In June and July 2019, my team of four conducted an interesting theoretical study on FOREX options in a perpetual-time setting. With typical, finite-time American call options, for instance, one will not exercise them early when the prevailing domestic interest rate is positive. But in the FOREX case, not exercising means losing out on the quote currency's interest rate. How then to proceed? We isolated the effect of time to ask how one would optimally exercise perpetual FOREX options - the exercise threshold would be some "stock price" (to be precise, some exchange rate) that depends only on the domestic and foreign interest rates, as well as market volatility. Using mathematical reasoning and numerical simulation, we obtained various results under different market assumptions. Please find a working paper on the topic below:
